4. Finance Commentary Draft

Highlights

  • Urgent Couriers outperformed FY26 budget on both revenue and NPBT.
  • March delivered a positive month for Urgent Couriers, with NPBT of $136.6k.
  • Urgent Cool held March operating expenses below budget, indicating some cost discipline despite the weak result.
  • DFRNT NZ operating expenses closed below budget for FY26.

Lowlights

  • Urgent Cool finished FY26 materially behind budget on both revenue and profitability.
  • Group March GP% finished below budget, driven by weaker conversion outside Urgent Couriers.
  • DFRNT remains heavily investment-weighted, with operating losses continuing ahead of meaningful recurring revenue.
  • The balance sheet section in the workbook appears not to have been updated to March and needs refresh before board circulation.

Division commentary — starter draft

Urgent Couriers: Strong FY26 trading year overall, with trading income ahead of budget by $960.6k. Other income also ran ahead of budget. Profitability remained solid despite March GP being slightly softer than ideal.

Urgent Cool: The business remained under pressure through FY26, primarily from revenue underperformance rather than uncontrolled overheads. March commentary in the workbook notes a negative EBITDA result impacted by the PMY522 engine failure, with an indication that EBITDA would otherwise have been positive.

DFRNT: DFRNT continued as a strategic investment line, with low current-period revenue and ongoing build costs. Any board discussion should clearly separate platform investment, commercial pipeline, and timing of revenue realisation.

5. Operating Highlights

  • Sales finished 5% ahead of budget for the year, driven in part by winning LifeHealthcare business at approximately $80k/month.
  • Woop received a 7% price increase effective mid-April.
  • New business noted includes Chicken and Things (~$30k/month), Inex Metals (~$5k/month), Qantas Courier (~$5k–10k/month), and Toyota Manukau (~$10k/month).
  • Rotorua regular work from World Courier and Marken was noted as improving route economics.
  • P2P was described as consistent through the quarter, with dispatch and CS teams materially happier following ongoing system fixes.
  • After-hours top-ups were reported at only $1,127 for the three months.
  • Warehouse handover to Kiwicold, Wonky Box and Hyper Ride was completed during the quarter.

6. Operational Challenges

  • JR remains a commercial risk pending contract certainty.
  • Home Delivery revenue continues to churn toward competitors, creating pressure on those services.
  • Smartways is discounting aggressively, particularly around Zimmer.
  • Some regional runs, including Whakatane and Taupo, remain challenged for sustainable driver earnings.
  • DIFOT / late-call / courier sustainability reporting gaps continue to limit KPI visibility.
  • New warehouse tenant activity has increased traffic flow and H&S complexity on site.
  • Urgent Cool faced significant service strain from road closures and an engine failure on PMY522.

7. Health and Safety

Urgent Couriers: the H&S tab records 1 high-risk and 1 medium-risk incident in January 2026. No incidents were reported in February or March 2026, which the workbook flags as a reporting-discipline concern. Ongoing focus area remains the traffic management plan following the addition of new tenants.

Urgent Cool: no March incident count was clearly flagged in the visible summary. The visible sheet appears centred on February 2026 meeting content rather than a clean March board summary.
